What is a passkey?

Passkeys are a secure alternative to password.

There is no need to remember or enter it when logging in.

It is a digital key that is created and safely stored on your device.

You will be able to log in using your fingerprint, face, PIN or passcode or any other method you use to unlock your device.

Why you should use a passkey

Passkeys provide the strongest protection against phishing and hacking attempts.

Each passkey is unique and linked to the website or app it is created for. Fraudulent apps or websites cannot steal your passkey.

Your passkey is safely stored on your device. No one can see or access it, including NHS login. It cannot be leaked even if a website or app is hacked.

What you need to set up a passkey

To set up a passkey you should have a screen lock on your device. A screen lock can be your fingerprint, face, passcode, PIN or pattern.

If you're using an Apple device, you will need to enable iCloud Keychain. This way your passkey will be synched and available across all your Apple devices.

You also need to have Bluetooth enabled on your device. This will allow you to set up a passkey on a nearby device.

How to set up a passkey

You can set up a passkey on your phone, computer, laptop or tablet.

You may also set up a passkey on a USB security key.

Important:

You should only set up passkeys on your personal or trusted devices. When you create a passkey on a device, anyone who can unlock the device can log into your account using your passkey.

Set up a passkey on the device that you are using

  1. visit Login and security settings in your NHS account
  2. select Passkeys under Login and security settings
  3. select Set up a passkey and a pop up will display, asking you to create a passkey

    If you are on a desktop device, you may be asked to choose where you want to save your passkey. To save your passkey on the device that you are using, select 'This device' or similar. If you are using a Windows device, select 'Windows Hello'.

  4. select 'Continue' and your device will ask you to authenticate by using your fingerprint, face, passcode or pattern or any other method you use to unlock your device
  5. follow the onscreen instructions and complete passkey set up

Set up a passkey on a different device than the one you are using

For example, you may be on your laptop but you might want to save your passkey on your mobile phone or tablet.

For this, you should have your Bluetooth enabled on both of your devices.

  1. visit Login and security settings in your NHS account
  2. select Passkeys under Login and security settings
  3. select Set up a passkey and a pop up will display, asking you to create a passkey
  4. select Use a different phone or tablet or similar and a QR code will display

    If you are on a mobile phone or using Safari browser, you might first need to select Other options or similar.

  5. scan the QR code using the device that you want to save your passkey on
  6. follow the onscreen instructions to connect your devices
  7. your device will ask you to authenticate by using your fingerprint, face, passcode or pattern or any other method you use to unlock your device
  8. follow the onscreen instructions to authenticate and complete passkey set up

Set up a passkey on a Windows desktop

  1. visit Login and security settings in your NHS account
  2. select Passkeys under Login and security settings
  3. select Set up a passkey and a pop up will display, asking you to create a passkey
  4. to set up your passkey on your Windows desktop, you should select Windows Hello or external security key
  5. your device will ask you to authenticate using your fingerprint or face or your PIN. This is your Windows Hello PIN that you use to unlock your device
  6. follow the onscreen instructions to authenticate and complete passkey set up

How to log in with a passkey

Log in on a device that has your passkey

  1. select email input field to enter your email address

    Your device might offer autofill to log in with your passkey.

  2. you can select to autofill, or enter your email address and select Continue
  3. your device will ask you to authenticate by using your fingerprint, face, passcode or pattern or any other method you use to unlock your device
  4. follow the onscreen instructions to authenticate and complete logging in

Log in on a device that does not have your passkey

For example, you may have set up your passkey on your mobile phone or tablet but you might want to log in on a laptop.

  1. enter your email address and select to continue and a pop up will display, asking you to log in with your passkey
  2. select Use a different phone or tablet or similar and a QR code will display
  3. scan the QR code using your phone or tablet that has your passkey
  4. follow the onscreen instructions to authenticate and complete logging in

Synch and use your passkey across your devices

If you set up your passkey on an iPhone or iPad, it will be synched and available across all your Apple devices that are logged into the same Apple ID.

If you set it up on an Android phone, it will be synched and available on other Android devices that are logged into the same Google Account.

You can set up a passkey separately on your desktop, on your tablet and on your mobile phone.

You can have multiple passkeys set up on different devices.

If you lose your device

Your passkey is protected by your device's screen lock. To use a passkey, your device will ask for your fingerprint, face, passcode or pattern to unlock your device. Without this, others cannot use your passkey and it will remain secure on the device.

You can still log into your account using your password and a security code sent to your phone. You can also log in using a another device that has your passkey.

If you change to a new device

Important:

Make sure to remove your passkey from your old device. You can do this by using the device or browser settings.

If you change your mobile phone or tablet

If you are using the same Apple ID or Google account, your passkey will be automatically synched and available on your new device. You will not need to create a new passkey.

Otherwise, you can set up a new passkey using Login and security settings in your NHS account.

If you change your laptop or computer

If you are changing your laptop or computer, you will need to create a new passkey.

You can set up a new passkey using Login and security settings in your NHS account.
